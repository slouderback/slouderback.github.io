Sources

Why we create

Tanner Christensen

Christensen, Tanner. “Why We Create.” Creative Something, Creative Something, 26 June 2017, https://creativesomething.net/post/30519924444/why-we-create.

This page is a collection of thoughts of the author around why people choose to create. Their claims are that people create for a variety of reason from emotions, desire for calm, and even money. I chose this source because it gives me a good bouncing off point for my data collection methods of what to expect and once I have data I can compare my findings to their claims.

Tanner Christensen has their own bio "Inspiring creative thinking and ideas since 2008. Tanner Christensen shares insights and ideas covering everything from art, psychology, and the neuroscience of creative thinking."

US Media Consumption trends: Tectonic shifts emerge

What's New in Publishing

What's New In Publishing. “US Media Consumption Trends: Tectonic Shifts Emerge: What's New in Publishing: Digital Publishing News.” US Media Consumption Trends: Tectonic Shifts Emerge, 23 Sept. 2021, https://whatsnewinpublishing.com/us-media-consumption-trends-tectonic-shifts-emerge/.

This article is a data presentation of statistics about media consumption in America regardfing things like platform, age, and paid vs unpaid consumption. This is useful for me because it not only introduced new aspects of media consumption I hadnt thought of but also provides new data to compare mine to at the end of this project.

What's New in Publishing is more of a summary source for newly published data. In this page they are going over the "US Media Consumption Report 2021" conducted by Attest. I went for this source over the original report because this is much more digestable and hihglights key points. However its important to also maintain a link to the original data for future use.

What Kind of Art is Popular Today? (Styles & Mediums)

Bryan Mullennix

Mullennix, Bryan. “What Kind of Art Is Popular Today? (Styles & Mediums).” ArtistryFound.com, 5 Jan. 2022, https://artistryfound.com/what-kind-of-art-is-popular-today-styles-mediums/.

This source covers art trends occuring today. It goes over popular art styles and discusses their role in todays art climate, technologies role in art and how it can be used as a medium, and concludes with how old school art mediums are still popular today. This source fills a gap for me regarding classical "art". The previous source talks about media/news usage and this one covers art usage.

The author of this is Bryan Mullennix, a commercial photographer who has semi retired and gone back to "enjoy art for arts sake". He has spent his whole career in this field watching it evolve and thus can provide useful insight into trends over recent years.
