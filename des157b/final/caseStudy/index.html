Project Overview

This project will explore how different generations use media today and how they choose to create new media themselves. The project will be designed for both web and mobile thus requiring it to be responsive to all sizes.

I will use chart.js to create the charts/plots such as bar graphs, pie charts, and doughnut charts. For color I plan on allowing different color schemes as a user choice however the format will be a two color scheme. One color will be a primary and the other will be the accent.

Project Research

I looked at a lot of other websites that had a sort of inforgraphic style to them for inspiration and understanding of best practices for this type of site.

I noticed there was a lot of focus on glance value such that a user could take a quick glance at your graphs and get an idea of whats going on. Also consistent color usage was really key for this and for understanding of the data when you start to examine it over time.

App Design

I wanted to create a layout similar to stripes page. I have always really liked their design style and I think how they allow images to appear above everything else is an amazing technique.

With that in mind I made the form on the landing page try to look as if it was above everything else. However I wanted to graphs to be the center of the rest of the site so I went for a very minimalist style throughout the whole site but even more post page one. The color choice for the graphs was something I spent a long time testing and trying to find the right ones. The thing I wanted was for the color to really represent the medium it was attached to while still being a set of colors that looked good together.

Tasks For Users

Naviagation was left extermely simple because I have never found sites with complex naviagation to be something I found as a benifit. The one expection to this for me is if your site is a store then having a "catalogue" drop down menu flow makes sense.

However since this site isnt that I left the naviagation to be just scrolling down and clicking through pages on a form. There is more subtle navigation within the charts themselves when you hover certain over certain parts but none of that is central to the site so im ok with leaving it in.

Usability Testing & Further Development

Usability was mostly focused in the form because form progression can be a huge driver of users leaving your site. Even though the form isnt required I was worried if someone started it then gave up halfway through the logical things to do would be close the site. For this reason I made the form a two page flow and included the tracker at the top.

The other major thing I noticed from usability was that while there was a lot of work that had to be done to generate the graphs they dont take more than a few seconds to fully understand for the user. Because of this I went back and added in that second page of the form to be able to generate more later content and also allow some direct way for users to leave their response at a length that suits them. One of the most interesting questions in the form is the final question that allows users to write down anything that comes to mind thus leading to a style of answers I no longer control. Eventually I want to build a function that auto generates a word cloud from that data.

Summary

From this project I learned how data can have a lot going into it yet produce such a small piece of content for your site. It made me appreciate those inforgraphics that go viral on social media so much more because I now have context for understanding how much data they needed to use and condense.

In this project im most proud of the charts being automatically updated as the form gets more and more responses. I dont need to do anything for the data to get updated and shown in the graphs so in theory this could continute to "update" even without my action.

The most challenging thing for this was finding a way to make this kind of site look interesting. I dont think I really got it right but I definitely improved the quality of it through development.
